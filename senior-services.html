Scaling HR Operations Through Automated Workflows

I took a high-touch, manual compliance process and turned it into a self-sustaining learning platform that manages employee training from start to finish.

My role: Product Lead AI Design Engineer Systems Design

Project at a Glance

The Problem: A single administrator was manually managing employee training using spreadsheets and manual follow-ups. This created a significant operational bottleneck, resulting in 12 full workdays of "busy work" annually and a $20,000+ liability risk due to potential gaps in compliance auditing.

The Action: I acted as the solo product lead, designer, and orchestrator to build a custom, automated compliance portal. Using an AI-assisted "vibe coding" workflow, I moved from high-level requirements to a production-ready system that features automated compliance guardrails and a zero-maintenance architecture.

The Result (Estimated): With the system in the final stages before launch, we anticipate a 70-80% reduction in administrative time and 100% audit accuracy through automated tracking. Additionally, the custom build bypassed $2,400 in annual software fees, providing a sustainable, professional tool at $0/month.

The Context

Senior Services of South Sound is a non-profit dedicated to improving the lives of seniors through essential services and community connection. Because they operate with a lean staff and no dedicated tech team, their internal processes had to be extremely efficient to keep up with strict state regulations.

The Challenge: Automating a Manual Training Workflow

I focused on a specific bottleneck: the manual tracking of employee education and compliance. The existing process relied on one person to manage files, update spreadsheets, and manually chase staff for completion records.

  • High-Risk Liability: Without a single "source of truth," the organization risked $20k+ in fines if their records were found incomplete during a state audit.
  • Wasted Capacity: The HR Director was spending roughly 8 hours a month on manual follow-ups — amounting to 12 workdays a year lost to administrative "busy work."
  • Fragmentation: Data was scattered across various folders and email threads, making it impossible to get a real-time view of certification status.

The Approach: From Large Teams to Solo Ownership

Transitioning from large-scale tech teams to a solo environment required a fundamental shift in my approach. Without a squad of PMs or engineers, I took full ownership of the entire lifecycle—from sitting down with the HR Director to map out their needs, to building the technical blueprints for how the system functions.

I delivered a three-part platform designed to balance the compliance requirements with a seamless experience:

The Admin "Command Center"

I spent a lot of time on the Admin Dashboard to ensure it functioned as a true command center where the HR Director can see all employees at a glance, assign training, and track progress in real-time. It’s designed to handle the "heavy lifting" of technical architecture and compliance rules in the background so the front-end stays simple.

×

The Compliance Engine

To make the system "audit-proof," I built in automated guardrails that allow the system to essentially audit itself.

  • Enforced Viewing: I included mandatory minimum timers that prevent users from clicking through to the end, ensuring they meaningfully engage with the content.
  • Knowledge Verification: I made it easy for admins to build and add interactive quizzes to the end of any training to verify that staff actually absorbed the information.
  • Audit Defense: The system creates immutable, time-stamped acknowledgment records for every completed module, providing a solid defense in case of a state audit.
×

The Employee Portal

Because this team is busy caring for seniors, they don't have time to struggle with a confusing interface. I focused on a "frictionless" design with high-accessibility standards (WCAG) and generous tap targets for use on any device.

  • Clear Visibility: Staff can instantly see their required quizzes and upcoming due dates.
  • Supportive Micro-copy: I spent time on the wording to ensure it felt supportive rather than demanding, helping staff get their requirements done fast so they can get back to their actual jobs.

The Result

By handling the technical architecture and compliance logic myself, I was able to ensure the system handles the boring stuff in the background. Everything is backed by automatic tracking—the second an employee finishes a quiz, the system updates their records instantly, removing the need for any manual data entry or human follow-up.

The Strategy

While I typically collaborate with engineers to bring a vision to life, this project required me to bridge that gap myself. By treating AI as a high-level technical partner, I moved beyond static designs to engineer a production-ready tool from the ground up.

Evolution of a New Workflow: "Vibe Coding"

To deliver on my goal of mastering AI-first development, I used a "Vibe Coding" workflow that matured as the complexity of the project grew:

  1. Learning the Patterns: I started with broad prompts, but quickly realized they led to fragile code that didn't meet my standards.
  2. Guiding the Strategy: I shifted to using Gemini as a high-level logic partner—vetting my plans in plain English to ensure the technical architecture was sound before providing instructions to the building tool.
  3. Driving the Execution: Toward the end, I was fully in the driver's seat. I developed a Governance Framework (a set of rules for privacy, accessibility, and engineering best practices) and moved from prompting to performing direct code edits for total control.

Building a People-First, Zero-Maintenance Solution

I didn't just want to build a tool; I wanted to build a sustainable system that could survive without a dedicated tech team. This required a focus on three core technical pillars:

  • Compliance Guardrails: I engineered technical "locks" like mandatory presentation timers, interactive quizzes, and immutable, time-stamped acknowledgments. This ensures the organization meets regulatory standards by default, removing the risk of human error.
  • Inclusive & Clear Design: Using WCAG accessibility standards and generous "tap targets" for mobile use, I made sure the interface was frictionless for staff who are busy caring for seniors and don't have time for a steep learning curve.
  • Robust, Zero-Cost Stack: To ensure long-term sustainability, I chose a professional-grade stack including Supabase (backend/database) and Google Auth (identity). This allows the platform to run with enterprise-level security and zero maintenance for $0/month.

Estimated Success Metrics

We’re in the final steps before launch, and the projected impact is significant. Based on the previous manual workload of around 8 hours a month:

  • 70-80% Time Savings: By automating tracking and notifications, I eliminated the 8 hours a month previously spent on manual follow-ups, freeing up 12 workdays a year for high-impact strategic work.
  • 100% Audit Accuracy: The system eliminates human error in data entry, ensuring every completion is accurately timestamped and recorded.
  • Zero-Cost Sustainability: By leveraging professional-grade free tiers, I managed to bypass the $2,400 annual cost of a typical commercial system. The result is a professional, sustainable tool that costs the non-profit $0/month to run.

Final Reflection

This was my first experience with "Vibe Coding," and I’m honestly blown away by how quickly I could turn a vision into a fully functional tool. It was incredibly satisfying to take full ownership of the build, and I’m excited to keep using this workflow to bring more ideas to life.

Key Takeaways

  • Collaborative Workflow: I learned to communicate with the AI as a logic partner rather than just a task-runner. By moving beyond basic prompts and establishing clear standards, I could vet complex plans and maintain high quality throughout the build.
  • New Perspective on Engineering: I became much more familiar with how engineers structure data and best practices for building scalable software, which has improved how I communicate with technical teams.
  • Art of Balance: I learned to balance feature requests against time, costs, and complexity, ensuring the tool remains easy for the non-profit to manage in the future.

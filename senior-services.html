Staff Training Portal Built for Audit-Ready Compliance

I designed and built a training platform for a lean non-profit that automates compliance tracking and runs at $0/month.

My role: Product Lead AI-Assisted Development Compliance Design

Project at a Glance

Problem: Manual training management created 12 workdays of unnecessary overhead annually and a $20,000+ compliance liability. The solution had to meet regulatory compliance standards, run at zero cost, and remain fully self-maintaining for a non-technical team.

My Role: Solo product lead, designer, and engineer across the full lifecycle, from requirements to production launch.

Action: Built a custom training portal with AI-assisted development, featuring automated compliance guardrails and a zero-maintenance architecture.

Result (Projected): 70-80% reduction in administrative time, 100% audit accuracy, and $0/month operating cost.

The Context

Senior Services of South Sound is a non-profit dedicated to improving the lives of seniors through essential services and community connection. Because they operate with a lean staff and no dedicated tech team, their internal processes had to be extremely efficient to keep up with strict state regulations.

The Challenge

The real challenge was designing a single system that worked for three very different audiences: regulators, staff, and a non-technical admin team.

  • Wasted Capacity: A single administrator managed all employee training manually, averaging 8 hours a month of unnecessary overhead for an HR Director who wears many hats.
  • Compliance Risk: Data scattered across folders and email threads made it impossible to get a real-time view of certification status, creating a $20k+ liability risk if records were found incomplete during a state audit.

The Constraints

The solution had to work within four non-negotiable boundaries:

  • Zero budget: As a lean non-profit, the system had to match professional-grade quality at $0/month.
  • No dedicated tech team: Fully self-maintaining and handover-ready for a semi-technical operator.
  • Regulatory accountability: Real audit risk from funders, accreditors, and regulators, with no centralized system to demonstrate compliance.
  • Non-technical user base: Staff have demanding jobs. Even mandatory training deserved a frictionless, respectful experience.

The Approach

Transitioning from large-scale tech teams to a solo environment required a fundamental shift in my approach. Without a squad of product managers or engineers, I took full ownership of the entire lifecycle, from sitting down with the HR Director to map out their needs, to building the technical blueprints for how the system functions.

The Build

I used an AI-assisted development workflow that evolved as the project grew. I started in Lovable to move fast on the initial architecture, then transitioned to Claude Code and direct code editing as the compliance requirements grew in complexity.

Deliverables extended beyond the UI, including a formal IT handover document covering compliance rationale, troubleshooting procedures, and developer notes to ensure the system could be maintained without me.

Centralized Administrator Portal

The admin dashboard gives the HR Director a real-time view of every staff member’s training status, with the compliance logic running silently in the background.

  • Staff Records: When a staff member leaves, their account is archived rather than deleted. Access is removed immediately, but their full training history is permanently retained for compliance.
  • Data Export: Admins can download a full record of every staff member’s training history, including completion dates, quiz results, and attestation status, providing a ready-made compliance record for audits and regulatory reviews.

The Compliance Engine

The system enforces compliance automatically, removing the risk of human error from the audit trail.

  • Enforced Viewing: Mandatory minimum timers prevent staff from clicking through to the end, ensuring meaningful engagement with the content.
  • Knowledge Verification: Admins can build and attach interactive quizzes to any training module to verify staff understanding.
  • Audit Defense: The system creates immutable, time-stamped acknowledgment records for every completed module, providing a solid defense in case of a state audit.
  • Quiz Versioning: If quiz questions are updated after a training has already been assigned, the system automatically creates a new version. Existing assignments are preserved as-is, only new assignments receive the updated version. This ensures audit records always reflect the exact questions each person answered.
  • Randomized Question Order: Questions are presented in a different order for every attempt, discouraging answer-sharing between staff and helping verify genuine understanding of the material.

The Employee Portal

Designed for staff with demanding jobs, the portal prioritizes clarity and accessibility. WCAG standards ensure it works for anyone, on any device.

  • Clear Visibility: Staff can instantly see their required quizzes and upcoming due dates.
  • Supportive Micro-copy: Wording throughout the portal is intentionally supportive rather than demanding, helping staff complete their requirements and get back to their work.
  • Assignment Emails: When a training is assigned, staff receive an automated email notification. This creates a verifiable record that training was communicated, not just assigned in the system.
  • Audit Log: Every significant staff action, from opening a training to signing an attestation, is automatically recorded in a tamper-evident audit log. No manual entry required.

Projected Impact

Based on the previous manual workload of 8 hours a month:

  • 70-80% reduction in administrative time: Automating tracking and notifications eliminates the manual follow-up cycle, freeing up an estimated 12 workdays a year for higher-impact work.
  • 100% audit accuracy: Every completion is automatically timestamped and recorded, eliminating human error from the compliance trail.
  • $0/month operating cost: By building on professional-grade free tiers, the system bypasses $2,400 in annual software fees with no compromise on security or reliability.

Final Reflection

This project represents a shift in what’s possible for a product designer working solo. By leveraging AI-assisted development, I was able to design, build, and launch a production-ready system end-to-end, something that would have required an entire engineering team in a traditional workflow.

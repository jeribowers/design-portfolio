Scaling HR Operations Through Automated Workflows

I took a high-touch, manual compliance process and turned it into a self-sustaining learning platform that manages employee training from start to finish.

My role: Product Lead AI Technical Orchestrator Systems Design

Project at a Glance

The Problem: A single administrator was manually managing employee training using spreadsheets and manual follow-ups, leading to high administrative overhead and risks of missed requirements.

The Action: I acted as the solo product lead, designer, and orchestrator to build a custom, automated portal. Using an AI-assisted "vibe coding" workflow, I moved from a Product Requirements Document (PRD) to a fully functional, integrated system.

The Result (Estimated): A 70-80% reduction in administrative time and 100% audit accuracy through automated tracking and notifications.

The Context

Senior Services of South Sound is a non-profit organization dedicated to improving the lives of seniors through essential services and community connection.

The Challenge: Automating a Manual Training Workflow

I focused on solving a specific operational bottleneck: employee education and compliance. Previously, the organization relied on a manual lifecycle where one person tracked progress, managed files, and followed up with staff. I transformed this into a centralized system that removes the need for manual tracking, allowing the administrator to work on more impactful projects while ensuring all staff stay up to date.

The Role: Solo Product Orchestrator

This project was a significant departure from my typical work within large tech teams. Without a dedicated squad of engineers or product managers, I was responsible for the entire lifecycle — from gathering requirements with the Deputy Executive Director to defining the data logic and integrating third-party services.

I delivered a dual-sided platform designed to handle the high-stakes needs of compliance while remaining simple for the end-user.

  • Employee Portal: A streamlined view for staff to access required trainings, track due dates, and complete quizzes.
  • Admin Dashboard: A direct management interface to oversee the employee roster, assign content, and review real-time progress.

The Strategy

Evolution of a New Workflow: "Vibe Coding"

Typically, I collaborate with an engineering team to bring designs to life. For this project, I learned how to bridge that gap myself using AI as a high-level collaborator. My process evolved in three stages:

  1. Beginner Phase: Initially, I used broad prompts expecting the AI to fill in the gaps. This was inefficient and resulted in fragile code that didn't follow the quality standards I wanted.
  2. Conversational Logic Phase: I shifted to a deep, back-and-forth dialogue with the AI. I used Gemini as a logic partner to vet my plans in plain English first, which allowed me to provide much more surgical instructions to the building tool.
  3. Advanced Implementation Phase: I developed a comprehensive System Guide that acted as a set of rules for privacy, accessibility, and engineering best practices. As I became more comfortable with the code, I moved from prompting to performing direct edits myself, allowing for much finer control over the final product.

Designing for a People-First Organization

The employees are often less tech-savvy, so the experience needed to feel like a helpful assistant rather than a hurdle.

  • Conversational Compliance: I focused heavily on supportive micro-copy, replacing robotic alerts with friendly language.
  • Visual Guardrails: I built-in specific cues like presentation timers and locked "Acknowledgment" sections that guide users through their requirements naturally.

Driving Efficiency through Integrated Services

To make the system "unbreakable" for an organization without a dev team, I integrated professional services to handle the heavy lifting:

  • Automated Communication: I integrated Resend to automatically email employees when a training is assigned, removing the manual follow-up burden.
  • Secure, One-Click Access: I implemented Google Auth to restrict access to company emails, providing a secure and familiar login experience for all staff.

The Impact

Estimated Success Metrics

Based on the previous manual workload of 15–20 hours a month:

  • 70-80% Time Savings: Calculated by the shift to a system where tracking and notifications happen automatically without human intervention.
  • 100% Audit Accuracy: The system eliminates human error in data entry, ensuring every completion is accurately timestamped and recorded.
  • Zero-Cost Sustainability: By leveraging professional-grade free tiers, the portal costs the non-profit $0/month to run.

Lessons Learned

  • Mastering Vibe Coding: As my first project using this workflow, I learned how to move from vision to a live product by treating AI as a "junior dev" that needs clear, system-level guidance.
  • New Perspective on Engineering: I became much more familiar with how engineers structure data and best practices for building scalable software, which has improved how I communicate with technical teams.
  • Art of Balance: I learned to balance feature requests against time, costs, and complexity, ensuring the tool remains easy for the non-profit to manage in the future.
